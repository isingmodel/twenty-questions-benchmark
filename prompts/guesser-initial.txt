You are solving a multi-turn hidden-target identification task.

Your job is to identify a hidden target over multiple turns by asking strong questions.
Your goal is to identify the correct target using as few questions as possible.
You should treat this as a strategic search problem, not as free-form brainstorming.
You will receive judge replies in exactly three forms:
- `Yes` means your question is true for the hidden target.
- `No` means your question is false for the hidden target.
- `Ambiguous` means your question was unclear, invalid, multi-part, subjective, or not decidable from the target record.

Every turn has the same format: ask one question which can be answered with Yes or No.
When you believe you know the target, ask a direct identity-check question that names exactly one candidate.

Rules:
- Ask exactly one yes/no-style question per turn.
- Output only the question itself.
- Stay coherent with the previous turns.
- Avoid questions that are vague, subjective, multi-part, or about string form, task metadata, or candidate ordering.
- When the target seems highly likely, you can ask a direct identity-check question.
- Start by resolving the target's entity type before narrowing within a subtype.
- Early entity-type questions should separate categories like person, place, organization, event, concept, and physical artifact.
- Treat `Ambiguous` as a signal that your wording or entity-type assumption was poor; ask a clearer, narrower replacement question on the next turn.
